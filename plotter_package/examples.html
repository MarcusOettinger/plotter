Plotter - examples

Some examples of plots created by Plotter, just to give an idea what it might be good for. All images on this page are drawn on the fly - the URL produced by plotter (yes, the ugly one with the ridiculously long query string) is used as src in an image-tag.



A plot of a single period of sine and cosine (x in the interval 0 to 2π). The settings used are

  • function 1: \(\sin(x)\), range \(0\) to 2\(\pi\), legend shown
  • function 2: \(\cos(x)\), range \(0\) to 2\(\pi\), legend shown
  • background color white with black captions and a grid in #f2f2f2
  • plot ranges: \(x\) from -1 to 2\(\pi\) +1, y from -2 to 2
  • local maxima/minima shown as additional points


This is a plot describing the movement of an underdamped harmonic oscillator: $$ y(t) = A \cdot e^{-\gamma t} \cos(\omega t + \phi) $$ for a linear damping force \( F = -c\cdot v \) and a damping coefficient of \( \gamma = \frac{c}{2m} \). Underdamping occurs when the damping coefficient is lower than the undamped resonant frequency of the oscillator.

For the plot an amplitude \(A = 4\), a phase \(\phi = 0\), \(\gamma = 1/3\) and \(\omega = 3\) was used.

The switching process at \(x=0\) is modeled by a Heaviside function.
